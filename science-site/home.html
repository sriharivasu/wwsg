Astronomy

Biology

The study of living organisms, their structure, function, growth, and evolution, providing insights into the diversity of life on Earth.

Chemistry

The science of atomic matter, especially its chemical reactions, but also including its properties, structure, composition, behavior, and changes as they relate to the chemical reactions.

Computer Science

The study of computation, information, and automation, encompassing both theoretical and practical aspects.

Engineering

The application of science and mathematics to solve problems and create systems, structures, and technologies that improve efficiency and productivity.
